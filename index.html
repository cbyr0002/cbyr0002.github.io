Why This Program?

Research shows that 59% of young Victorians feel overwhelmed by the future impacts of climate change. Traditional education often inadvertently reinforces these fears without offering a path forward.

Our program utilizes a Whole-School Approach aligned with FISO 2.0. We shift the focus from passive worry to active, community-based solutions, integrating Indigenous knowledge to foster a deeper connection to Country.

Based on research by A. McLaine, C. Byron, & A. Kong (2025)

The 10-Hour Roadmap

A scaffolded journey from awareness to connection to action.

Phase 1: Immersion

Hours 1-5

Location: Zoo Excursion

2-3hrs Guided + 2hrs Free Roam. Students engage with biodiversity directly. The focus is on appreciating what we have, building the emotional foundation for conservation without the immediate pressure of "loss".

Phase 2: Context

Hours 6-10

Location: Classroom Modules

Bridging the gap. We connect the zoo experience to the upcoming camp. Students explore the scientific reality of climate change alongside the ethics of management, ensuring the excursion isn't just a "day off".

Phase 3: Connection

Camp Day 1

Location: School Camp

Key Focus: Indigenous culture & connection to Country. Through Yarning Circles, students explore how First Nations communities manage land and adapt to climate change.

Assessment: Final Camp Workbook Submission

High Impact Wellbeing Strategies (HIWS)

We don't just teach content; we support student health. Our 10-hour program is explicitly designed to hit all five High Impact Wellbeing Strategies.

1. Build a Positive School Environment

Program Link: The Zoo Excursion takes learning outside the classroom, fostering positive peer interactions and a shared sense of wonder and appreciation for nature.

2. Empower Student Voice & Agency

Program Link: The "Solution Focused" classroom modules move students from passive anxiety to active planning. The Camp Workbook requires personal reflection and solution-generation.

3. Promote Inclusion

Program Link: Our Camp module centers Indigenous perspectives and Yarning Circles, creating a culturally safe space that values diverse ways of knowing and being.

4. Respond to Wellbeing Concerns

Program Link: We explicitly name "Eco-Anxiety" in class, validating student feelings. Teachers are provided with a "Triage Guide" to identify students who may need Tier 2 or 3 support.

5. Partner with Families & Community

Program Link: The program connects school, zoo, and camp. The workbook includes a "Take Home" discussion section to bridge the gap between school learning and home conversations.
