Part A: Conversions

Fractions, decimals, and percentages are effectively three different languages for describing the same quantities‚Äîparts of a whole. Choosing the right form depends on the situation:

  • Fractions allow for exact precision (e.g., \( \frac{1}{3} \) is exact, whereas \( 0.333... \) requires rounding). They are preferred in algebra.
  • Decimals are the standard for currency, measurement, and digital input (calculators).
  • Percentages provide a universal standard (out of 100) that makes comparing different proportions, such as test scores or interest rates, much easier.

Key Conversion Rules:

  • Fraction → %: Multiply by 100.
  • Decimal → %: Multiply by 100 (move decimal 2 spots right).
  • % → Fraction: Put over 100 and simplify.
üë®‚Äçüè´ Worked Example 1: Conversions

1. Convert \( \frac{3}{8} \) to a percentage.

2. Convert 16% to a fraction.

1. \( \frac{3}{8} \times 100 = \frac{300}{8} = \mathbf{37.5\%} \)

2. \( \frac{16}{100} \). Divide top/bottom by 4 = \( \mathbf{\frac{4}{25}} \)

Part B: Percentage of a Quantity

When we talk about finding a "percentage of" something in mathematics, the word "of" almost always implies multiplication. To calculate this, we cannot multiply by the percentage number directly (e.g. 50). Instead, we must first convert the percentage into a usable number‚Äîeither a fraction or a decimal.

The Strategy:

  • Step 1: Convert the percentage to a decimal (divide by 100).
  • Step 2: Multiply the decimal by the total amount.

Formula: \( \text{Amount} \times \text{Decimal} \)

üë®‚Äçüè´ Worked Example 2: Calculation

Find 12% of $850.

Step 1: Convert 12% to decimal \( \rightarrow 0.12 \).

Step 2: Multiply. \( 850 \times 0.12 = \mathbf{\$102} \).

Q4. Convert Decimal to Fraction

Convert 12.5% to a simplified fraction.

Step 1: Write over 100. \( \frac{12.5}{100} \)

Step 2: Remove decimal (multiply both by 10). \( \frac{125}{1000} \)

Step 3: Simplify (divide by 125). \( \mathbf{\frac{1}{8}} \)

Q5. Complex Fraction Conversion

Convert the complex fraction \( \frac{3/4}{2.5} \) to a percentage.

Step 1: Convert \( \frac{3}{4} \) to 0.75.

Step 2: Divide: \( 0.75 \div 2.5 = 0.3 \).

Step 3: Convert to percentage: \( 0.3 \times 100 = \mathbf{30\%} \).

Part A: The Multiplier Method

The Multiplier Method is a powerful shortcut for increasing or decreasing a quantity by a percentage in a single step. Instead of calculating the specific dollar amount of the change and then adding or subtracting it, we adjust the "whole" (100%) directly.

This is particularly useful for financial calculations like interest or repeated discounts.

  • Increase by 20%: You start with 100% and add 20%. You now have 120%. As a decimal, this is 1.20.
  • Decrease by 15%: You start with 100% and remove 15%. You have 85% remaining. As a decimal, this is 0.85.
üë®‚Äçüè´ Worked Example 3: Discounts

A video game costs $60 and is discounted by 15%. Find the new price.

Step 1: Multiplier is \( 1 - 0.15 = 0.85 \).

Step 2: \( 60 \times 0.85 = \mathbf{\$51} \).

Part B: GST (Goods & Services Tax)

What is GST?

In Australia, the Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a broad-based tax of 10% on most goods, services and other items sold or consumed in Australia.

Where does it apply? It applies to most purchases including:

  • Electronics (phones, laptops)
  • Restaurant meals and takeaway food
  • Clothing and footwear
  • Services like plumbing, electrical work, and legal advice

Exceptions (GST-free): Most basic food (fresh fruit, vegetables, bread, milk), some education courses, and some medical, health and care products and services are GST-free.

Why do we have it? Businesses collect this extra 10% from customers and pay it to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). The money collected is distributed to the states and territories to help fund public services like schools, hospitals, and roads.

The Calculations:

  • Add GST (Calculate Price): Multiply by 1.1.
  • Remove GST (Find Original Cost): Divide by 1.1.
    (Caution: Do not subtract 10%!)
  • Find GST Component: Divide Final Price by 11.
üë®‚Äçüè´ Worked Example 4: Reverse GST

A plumber's invoice is $220 including GST. Find the pre-tax cost.

Step 1: Identify "Including GST" means we divide.

Step 2: \( 220 \div 1.1 = \mathbf{\$200} \).

Q9. Simple Increase

Increase $50 by 10%.

$$ 50 + 5 = \mathbf{\$55} $$

Q10. Population Growth

The population of a town is 12,000. It increases by 5% in one year. What is the new population?

Multiplier = 1.05.

$$ 12000 \times 1.05 = \mathbf{12,600} $$

Q11. Discount

A laptop is listed at $1,200 but is on sale for 20% off. Calculate the sale price.

Multiplier = 0.80 (paying 80%).

$$ 1200 \times 0.80 = \mathbf{\$960} $$

Q12. The Double Change (Trick)

A price is increased by 25%. The new price is then decreased by 20%. If the final price is $150, what was the original price?

Step 1: Check the total multiplier. \( 1.25 \times 0.80 = 1.0 \).

Step 2: This means the price did not change!

Answer: $150

Q13. Reverse Profit (Hard)

A store sells a bike for $550, making a 10% profit on the cost price. How much did the store buy the bike for?

The selling price represents 110% of the cost price.

$$ \text{Cost} = 550 \div 1.1 $$

Answer: $500

Multiply by 1.1.

$$ 20 \times 1.1 = \mathbf{\$22} $$

Q15. GST Component

Calculate the amount of GST included in a purchase of $110.

Divide final price by 11.

$$ 110 \div 11 = \mathbf{\$10} $$

Q16. Reverse GST

An electrician‚Äôs invoice is $440 (including GST). Calculate the pre-tax cost.

To remove GST, divide by 1.1.

$$ 440 \div 1.1 = \mathbf{\$400} $$

Q17. Mixed GST Basket

A shopping bill totals $160 (including GST). It includes $50 worth of fresh fruit (GST-free). Calculate the GST paid on the rest of the items.

Step 1: Identify taxable amount. \( 160 - 50 = \$110 \).

Step 2: Calculate GST on the $110. \( 110 \div 11 = \$10 \).

Answer: $10

Part A: Sharing Ratios

Ratios allow us to divide a total quantity into unequal shares. Think of a ratio like 3:7 as "shares" or "parts" of a whole pile.

  • Person A gets 3 parts.
  • Person B gets 7 parts.
  • Therefore, the total pile was divided into 3 + 7 = 10 parts.

To solve these problems, we determine the value of a single part first, and then multiply by the number of parts each person receives.

üë®‚Äçüè´ Worked Example 5: Sharing

Share $500 in the ratio 3:7.

Step 1: Total parts = \( 3+7=10 \).

Step 2: 1 part = \( 500 \div 10 = \$50 \).

Step 3: Shares are \( 3 \times 50 = \$150 \) and \( 7 \times 50 = \$350 \).

Part B: Unitary Method

The Unitary Method derives its name from the word "unit", meaning one. It is a logic-based approach used to solve problems where you are given the cost or value of a group (e.g., 5 apples) and need to find the value of a different quantity (e.g., 12 apples).

The Golden Rule: Always find the value of one single item first. Once you know the cost of one unit, you can calculate the cost of any number of units by multiplying.

üë®‚Äçüè´ Worked Example 6: Unitary Method

If 5 apples cost $3.50, how much do 12 apples cost?

Step 1: Find the cost of 1 apple (the unit). \( \$3.50 \div 5 = \$0.70 \).

Step 2: Multiply by the new quantity. \( 12 \times \$0.70 = \mathbf{\$8.40} \).

4. Ratios: Practice (Part A)

Q18. Simple Ratio

Simplify the ratio 10 : 20.

Divide both by 10.

\( \mathbf{1 : 2} \)

Q19. Simplify Ratio

Simplify the ratio 150 : 25 : 50.

Find the Highest Common Factor (25).

Divide all by 25: \( 6 : 1 : 2 \).

Q20. Unit Conversion

Convert the quantities to the same unit then simplify: 50 cm to 2 m.

Step 1: Convert to same unit (cm). \( 50 : 200 \).

Step 2: Divide by 50. \( \mathbf{1 : 4} \).

Q21. Ratio Difference

The ratio of Ann's savings to Bob's savings is 5:3. If the difference between their savings is $24, how much have they saved in total?

Step 1: Difference in parts = \( 5 - 3 = 2 \) parts.

Step 2: 2 parts = $24. So 1 part = $12.

Step 3: Total parts = \( 5 + 3 = 8 \).

Step 4: Total = \( 8 \times 12 = \mathbf{\$96} \).

4. Ratios: Practice (Part B)

Q22. Best Buy

Which is the better buy? 2 kg of rice for $6.50 or 500 g of rice for $1.80?

Compare cost per kg:

Option A: \( 6.50 \div 2 = \$3.25/\text{kg} \).

Option B: \( 1.80 \times 2 = \$3.60/\text{kg} \).

Option A is cheaper.

Q23. Concrete Mixing

Concrete is made using cement, sand, and gravel in the ratio 1:2:3. If you have 24 kg of mix total, how much sand is there?

Step 1: Total parts = \( 1+2+3 = 6 \).

Step 2: 1 part = \( 24 \div 6 = 4\text{kg} \).

Step 3: Sand is 2 parts. \( 2 \times 4 = \mathbf{8\text{kg}} \).

Q24. Fuel Consumption

A car travels 240km on 20L of fuel. How far can the same car travel on 8L of fuel?

Step 1: Find km per 1 Litre. \( 240 \div 20 = 12 \text{ km/L} \).

Step 2: Multiply by 8L. \( 12 \times 8 = \mathbf{96 \text{ km}} \).

Q25. Compound Ratio

The ratio of A:B is 2:3. The ratio of B:C is 4:5. Find the ratio of A:C.

Step 1: Make 'B' the same in both. LCM of 3 and 4 is 12.

A:B becomes \( (2\times4):(3\times4) = 8:12 \).

B:C becomes \( (4\times3):(5\times3) = 12:15 \).

Step 2: Combine. A:B:C = 8:12:15.

Answer A:C = 8:15
